## ğŸ“ Understanding the 5 Main Categories

### Category 1: Intrinsic Properties (What the food IS)

**Water Activity (aw):**
- The "free" water available for microbes
- Scale: 0.0 (bone dry) to 1.0 (pure water)
- Most bacteria can't grow below 0.90
- Most molds can't grow below 0.80

**pH (Acidity):**
- Scale: 0 (very acidic) to 14 (very alkaline)
- pH < 4.6 = safe from botulism
- Low pH = natural preservation

**Fat Content:**
- Higher fat = more prone to rancidity
- Oxidation is main concern
- Light and heat accelerate rancidity

### Category 2: Processing (What was DONE to it)

**Heat Processing:**
- Pasteurization: Kills most bacteria
- Sterilization: Kills all microorganisms
- UHT: Ultra-high temperature, long shelf life

**Dehydration:**
- Removes water to prevent microbial growth
- Lowers water activity
- Can be sun-dried, oven-dried, or freeze-dried

**Fermentation:**
- Uses beneficial microbes
- Produces acid or alcohol
- Natural preservation

**Freezing:**
- Stops microbial growth completely
- Slows chemical reactions
- Best at 0Â°F (-18Â°C) or below

### Category 3: Packaging (How it's PROTECTED)

**Hermetic (Airtight):**
- Cans, jars with vacuum seal
- Complete oxygen barrier
- Long shelf life (years)

**Modified Atmosphere (MAP):**
- Replace air with nitrogen/COâ‚‚
- Slows oxidation and microbial growth
- Extends shelf life 2-5x

**Vacuum:**
- Removes air/oxygen
- Prevents oxidation
- Extends shelf life 3-5x

### Category 4: Storage (Where/How it's KEPT)

**Temperature:**
- Most critical factor
- Qâ‚â‚€ rule: Every 10Â°C doubles reaction rates
- Refrigeration (32-40Â°F) is crucial for perishables

**Humidity:**
- High for fresh produce (prevents wilting)
- Low for dry goods (prevents mold)
- Match to food type

**Light:**
- UV damages vitamins
- Causes fat oxidation
- Dark storage is best

**Oxygen:**
- Causes rancidity in fats
- Enables aerobic bacteria
- Remove when possible

### Category 5: Microbial/Chemical (What can GO WRONG)

**Bacterial Growth:**
- Doubles every 20-30 minutes in ideal conditions
- Danger zone: 40-140Â°F (4-60Â°C)
- Cold slows, freezing stops

**Mold:**
- Grows on surfaces
- Needs oxygen
- Can grow slowly in fridge

**Oxidation:**
- Chemical reaction with oxygen
- Causes rancidity
- Accelerated by heat and light

**Enzyme Activity:**
- Natural enzymes in food
- Cause ripening, browning, softening
- Inactivated by heat (blanching)
